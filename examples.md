# Staged Assignment Examples

## Example 1: History - Primary Source Analysis

**STAGE 1: Close Reading and Description** (No AI)

*Learning Objectives:*
- Develop skills in careful textual analysis
- Practice descriptive observation without interpretation
- Build foundational understanding of historical documents

*AI Permissions: None (Human-Only Work)*

Students must complete this stage entirely on their own to develop genuine analytical reading skills.

*Assignment Description:*

Choose a primary source document from our course archive (letter, diary entry, newspaper article, speech, or government document from the period we're studying). Read it carefully multiple times.

Write 500-750 words describing what you observe in the document. What kind of document is it? Who created it? What does it say? What language, tone, or rhetorical strategies does it use? Note specific quotes and details. Focus on what's actually in the text—don't interpret or analyze yet, just describe what you see.

---

**STAGE 2: Historical Contextualization** (AI as Co-Intelligence)

*Learning Objectives:*
- Apply historical context and theoretical frameworks to primary sources
- Practice historical thinking by connecting documents to broader patterns
- Use AI as a research and analysis partner
- Develop critical evaluation skills when working with AI

*AI Permissions: Co-Intelligence Partner*

Students may use AI to help research historical context, explore historiographical debates, and test interpretative frameworks. Must submit Co-Intelligence Appendix documenting all AI interactions.

*Assignment Description:*

Return to your Stage 1 description. Now situate this document in its historical context. What was happening when this was created? Who was the intended audience? How does it reflect or challenge the period's dominant ideas, power structures, or conflicts? Apply relevant historical frameworks we've studied in class.

You may use AI to help research context, explore different historical interpretations, and test your analytical arguments. Document all AI use in your Co-Intelligence Appendix, showing how AI responses shaped your thinking.

Write 750-1000 words analyzing your document within its historical context.

---

**STAGE 3: Contemporary Resonance and Public History** (AI Extended Use with Visual)

*Learning Objectives:*
- Connect historical analysis to contemporary issues
- Develop public history communication skills
- Practice visual communication using AI tools
- Integrate analytical and creative modes of historical thinking

*AI Permissions: Extended Use with Image Generation*

Continue using AI as co-intelligence partner AND create a social media-style post with Gemini-generated image explaining the document's relevance today.

*Assignment Description:*

Based on your description (Stage 1) and historical analysis (Stage 2), now explore contemporary connections. How does this historical document speak to current debates, challenges, or questions? What can we learn from it today?

Write 500-750 words exploring contemporary resonance. Then create a social media-style educational post (Instagram/TikTok format) using Google Gemini to generate a compelling image that connects your historical document to modern issues.

Final submission includes:
- Written analysis of contemporary relevance
- Gemini-generated image for social media post
- The prompt used to create the image
- Brief explanation (200 words) of your public history communication strategy
- Complete Co-Intelligence Appendix covering all AI use in Stages 2 and 3

## Example 2: Biology - Scientific Research Project

**STAGE 1: Experimental Design and Data Collection** (No AI)

*Learning Objectives:*
- Design a controlled experiment using scientific method
- Collect and record raw data systematically
- Practice lab techniques and measurement accuracy
- Develop scientific observation and documentation skills

*AI Permissions: None (Human-Only Work)*

Students must design their experiment and collect data independently to develop authentic research skills.

*Assignment Description:*

Design and conduct a simple biological experiment exploring a testable hypothesis. This could investigate plant growth variables, bacterial growth rates, enzyme activity, osmosis, or another phenomenon we've covered.

Create a detailed lab notebook documenting:
- Your hypothesis and experimental design
- Materials and methods (written so others could replicate)
- Raw data tables with all measurements
- Observations during the experiment
- Any challenges or unexpected results

Do not analyze or interpret yet—just collect clean, accurate data. Submit 3-5 pages of lab documentation with your raw data.

---

**STAGE 2: Statistical Analysis and Interpretation** (AI as Co-Intelligence)

*Learning Objectives:*
- Apply statistical methods to experimental data
- Interpret results using biological principles
- Use AI to explore analytical approaches and verify calculations
- Evaluate AI-generated interpretations critically

*AI Permissions: Co-Intelligence Partner*

Students may use AI to help with statistical analysis, explore biological mechanisms, and test interpretations. Must document all AI use in Co-Intelligence Appendix.

*Assignment Description:*

Analyze your Stage 1 data. Calculate relevant statistics (means, standard deviations, t-tests, or other appropriate tests). Create graphs visualizing your results. Interpret what your data reveals about the biological phenomenon you studied.

You may use AI to:
- Help select appropriate statistical tests
- Verify calculations or troubleshoot spreadsheet formulas
- Explore biological mechanisms that might explain your results
- Generate alternative interpretations of unexpected findings

Write a 1000-1250 word Results and Discussion section following scientific paper format. Include your Co-Intelligence Appendix showing all AI interactions.

---

**STAGE 3: Research Communication and Visual Abstract** (AI Extended Use with Visual)

*Learning Objectives:*
- Communicate scientific findings to broader audiences
- Practice scientific writing for different contexts
- Create effective visual summaries using AI tools
- Connect research to real-world applications

*AI Permissions: Extended Use with Image Generation*

Continue using AI as analytical partner AND create a visual abstract using Gemini illustrating your research findings.

*Assignment Description:*

Transform your research into two communication formats:

1. **Lay Summary** (300-400 words): Explain your experiment, findings, and significance for a general audience (like a science news article). What did you discover and why does it matter?

2. **Visual Abstract**: Use Google Gemini to create a single-image visual abstract that captures your hypothesis, methods, key results, and conclusions. This should work standalone to communicate your research.

Final submission includes:
- Lay summary for general audiences
- Gemini-generated visual abstract
- The prompt used to generate the image
- Reflection (200 words) on how you adapted scientific content for different audiences
- Complete Co-Intelligence Appendix covering all AI use in Stages 2 and 3

## Example 3: Music - Performance Analysis and Interpretation

**STAGE 1: Active Listening and Descriptive Analysis** (No AI)

*Learning Objectives:*
- Develop critical listening skills through focused attention
- Practice musical description using appropriate terminology
- Build observational foundation before theoretical interpretation
- Strengthen aural skills and musical memory

*AI Permissions: None (Human-Only Work)*

Students must complete this stage independently to develop genuine listening and analytical skills.

*Assignment Description:*

Select a musical performance (live or recorded) of a piece we've studied this semester. Listen to it at least three times with full attention—no multitasking.

Write 600-800 words describing what you hear. Focus on concrete musical elements:
- Tempo, dynamics, articulation choices
- Instrumental/vocal tone quality and balance
- Phrasing decisions and breath/bow placement
- Specific moments that stand out
- How the performance unfolds over time

Describe what's actually happening in the music—don't interpret artistic intent or evaluate quality yet. Just document your detailed listening observations.

---

**STAGE 2: Interpretive Analysis and Performance Practice** (AI as Co-Intelligence)

*Learning Objectives:*
- Apply music theory and historical performance practice to interpretation
- Analyze artistic choices within stylistic conventions
- Use AI to explore multiple interpretive approaches
- Develop critical thinking about performance decisions

*AI Permissions: Co-Intelligence Partner*

Students may use AI to research performance practice traditions, compare interpretations, and explore theoretical concepts. Must submit Co-Intelligence Appendix.

*Assignment Description:*

Return to your Stage 1 observations. Now analyze the performer's interpretive choices. How do their decisions reflect (or challenge) historical performance practice for this style? What music theory concepts explain what you heard? How does this performance compare to other interpretations?

You may use AI to:
- Research historical performance practice conventions
- Explore theoretical analysis of the piece
- Compare different recorded interpretations
- Investigate the performer's artistic philosophy or training background

Write 1000-1200 words analyzing the performance within its musical, historical, and theoretical context. Include Co-Intelligence Appendix.

---

**STAGE 3: Personal Interpretation and Performance Portfolio** (AI Extended Use with Audio/Video)

*Learning Objectives:*
- Synthesize analysis into personal performance decisions
- Articulate and defend interpretive choices
- Document performance process using multimedia tools
- Connect analysis to embodied musical practice

*AI Permissions: Extended Use with Multimedia Creation*

Continue using AI as research partner AND use AI tools to create a performance portfolio (video editing, notation software, or audio visualization).

*Assignment Description:*

Based on your listening analysis (Stage 1) and interpretive research (Stage 2), now develop your own performance approach to a section of this piece.

Create a performance portfolio including:
- Short performance recording (2-3 minutes) demonstrating your interpretive choices
- Written statement (500-600 words) explaining specific artistic decisions and how they differ from or align with the recording you analyzed
- Annotated score/lead sheet showing your performance markings
- Optional: Use AI tools to create audio visualization, practice videos, or edited performance footage

Final submission includes your performance portfolio and Complete Co-Intelligence Appendix covering all AI use in Stages 2 and 3.

## Example 4: Business - Market Analysis and Strategy

**STAGE 1: Consumer Observation and Market Research** (No AI)

*Learning Objectives:*
- Conduct primary market research through direct observation
- Gather qualitative consumer insights
- Practice ethnographic business research methods
- Document market behavior without premature interpretation

*AI Permissions: None (Human-Only Work)*

Students must conduct original research to develop authentic market insight skills.

*Assignment Description:*

Select a specific consumer context to observe: a retail store, restaurant, coffee shop, gym, or online marketplace. Spend at least 3 hours observing across different times/days.

Document in 700-900 words:
- Who are the customers? (Demographics, behaviors, patterns)
- How do they interact with the product/service/space?
- What problems or needs do you observe?
- What surprises you?
- Peak times, bottlenecks, or friction points
- Actual customer statements you overhear

Focus on concrete observations—what you actually see and hear. Don't jump to business strategies yet.

---

**STAGE 2: Competitive Analysis and Business Model Evaluation** (AI as Co-Intelligence)

*Learning Objectives:*
- Apply business frameworks (Porter's Five Forces, SWOT, Business Model Canvas)
- Analyze competitive positioning and market dynamics
- Use AI to gather market intelligence and test strategic hypotheses
- Evaluate AI-generated business insights critically

*AI Permissions: Co-Intelligence Partner*

Students may use AI to research competitors, analyze industry trends, and explore strategic frameworks. Must submit Co-Intelligence Appendix.

*Assignment Description:*

Analyze your Stage 1 observations through business strategy frameworks. Who are the competitors? What's the business model? Where are opportunities or threats? What do industry trends suggest?

You may use AI to:
- Research competitor strategies and market positioning
- Gather industry data and trend analysis
- Apply business frameworks to your observations
- Generate alternative strategic interpretations

Write 1000-1250 words analyzing the business through strategic frameworks. Include competitive landscape, business model analysis, and market opportunities. Append Co-Intelligence documentation.

---

**STAGE 3: Strategic Recommendations and Pitch Deck** (AI Extended Use with Visual)

*Learning Objectives:*
- Synthesize research into actionable business strategy
- Develop persuasive business communication skills
- Create professional presentation materials using AI tools
- Practice strategic thinking and decision-making

*AI Permissions: Extended Use with Presentation Design*

Continue using AI as strategic partner AND use AI tools (Gemini, Canva, etc.) to create professional pitch deck visuals.

*Assignment Description:*

Based on your observations (Stage 1) and strategic analysis (Stage 2), develop specific business recommendations. What should this company do differently? What opportunities should they pursue?

Create:
- Written strategic brief (600-800 words) with 3-5 specific, actionable recommendations
- Professional pitch deck (6-8 slides) using AI-generated visuals to present your recommendations
- Implementation timeline and success metrics

Final submission includes:
- Strategic brief
- Pitch deck (PDF)
- Documentation of AI prompts used for visual creation
- Reflection (200 words) on your strategic decision-making process
- Complete Co-Intelligence Appendix covering all AI use in Stages 2 and 3
